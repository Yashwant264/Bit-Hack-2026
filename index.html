// About the Hackathon

What is BitHack?

BitHack '26 is a 36-hour innovation marathon organized by the Bit by Bit Club, the premier technical club of VIT Bhopal University. It's a platform where curious minds transform ideas into impactful solutions.

Whether you're a coder, designer, data scientist, or a problem-solver, BitHack is your arena to collaborate, create, and compete with the brightest tech minds on campus.

Teams will go through 3 structured stages — from ideation to final demo — tackling real-world challenges across multiple high-impact domains.

// Hackathon Journey

Three Stages

01
💡
Stage 01

IDEATION

Pitch your idea. Articulate the problem, propose your solution, and outline your approach. This is where big thinking begins.

  • Submit a problem statement within your chosen domain
  • Prepare a 2-page concept document (PDF)
  • Clearly define target users and impact
  • Include a basic wireframe or flow diagram
  • Teams evaluated on creativity & feasibility
  • Top 40 teams advance to Stage 2
02
🔧
Stage 02

PROTOTYPE

Build it. Code through the night, design the UX, test your logic. Transform your concept into a working prototype.

  • 24-hour coding sprint at the venue
  • Working prototype mandatory for evaluation
  • Mentors available for tech and domain guidance
  • Use any stack, framework, or tool of choice
  • Midpoint check-in at 12-hour mark
  • Top 10 teams advance to Final Stage
03
🚀
Stage 03

FINAL STAGE

Present to the jury. Demo your product, defend your choices, and show the judges why your solution wins.

  • 7-minute live demo presentation
  • 3-minute Q&A with expert panel jury
  • Judges evaluate impact, innovation & execution
  • Code quality and documentation are assessed
  • Business viability is a bonus criterion
  • Winners announced at closing ceremony

// Play Fair, Win Fair

Rules & Guidelines

⚙️ Team Rules

  1. Teams must consist of 2 to 5 members only.
  2. All team members must be registered students of VIT Bhopal.
  3. Each participant can be part of only one team.
  4. Team leader must coordinate with organizers for all communications.
  5. Team changes are not permitted after registration closes.
  6. Registration fee is ₹150 per member (non-refundable).

💻 Technical Rules

  1. All code must be written during the hackathon event window.
  2. Open-source libraries and public APIs are allowed.
  3. Pre-existing projects or templates are strictly prohibited.
  4. Teams must push code to GitHub — final commit timestamps will be verified.
  5. Plagiarism or code copying from other teams results in disqualification.
  6. AI tools may be used for assistance but not to generate the entire solution.

📋 Submission Rules

  1. Teams must submit a working GitHub repository link.
  2. A README file with setup instructions is mandatory.
  3. A demo video (max 3 minutes) must be submitted alongside code.
  4. Late submissions will not be accepted under any circumstances.
  5. Judging: Innovation (30%), Execution (30%), Impact (25%), Presentation (15%).

🏛️ Code of Conduct

  1. Participants must maintain respectful behavior towards all attendees.
  2. Harassment, discrimination, or misconduct will result in immediate disqualification.
  3. Organizers' decisions are final and binding in all matters.
  4. Participants are responsible for their own equipment and data.
  5. VIT Bhopal campus rules and regulations apply throughout.
